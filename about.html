About

Dr. Irina Owen is a licensed psychologist specializing in forensic evaluations with youth and adult clients. She received her doctorate from the APA accredited counseling psychology program at Northwest University and completed her APA accredited internship at Southeast Human Service Center in Fargo, North Dakota. Her areas of forensic evaluation expertise include competency/capacity to stand trial, not guilty by reason of insanity, diminished capacity/sentencing mitigation, civil commitment, juvenile transfer/waiver to adult court, sexual and violence risk assessment (for juveniles and adults), and school threat assessments. She has worked in community and detention settings, including schools, private practice, correctional facilities, and a forensic state hospital. Prior to her forensic work, Dr. Owen worked with court involved youth as a Court Appointed Special Advocate (CASA), and conducted a book club for youth in detention, both through Pierce County Juvenile Court in Washington state. She also sought to improve the lives of military youth and families by facilitating recreational activities for youth on Joint Base Lewis McChord, and completing her doctoral dissertation (Fortifying military relationships through Stronger Families) on the effectiveness of a marriage enrichment seminar program for active duty and veteran couples.
